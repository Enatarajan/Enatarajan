ABOUT EPULZ'25

EPULZ'25 is a prestigious national-level technical symposium organized by the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering at Thanthai Periyar Government Institute of Technology-Vellore.

This symposium aims to bring together brilliant minds from across India to showcase their technical prowess, innovative thinking, and problem-solving abilities through a series of challenging and engaging events.

With a focus on promoting creativity, technical innovation, and collaborative learning, EPULZ'25 provides a platform for students to network with industry professionals, academics, and peers while competing in cutting-edge technical and non-technical events.

TECHNICAL EVENTS

Techtalk Titans

A platform for aspiring engineers to showcase their presentation skills and technical knowledge. Participants will deliver concise talks on emerging technologies, innovative solutions, or research findings in the field of electronics and communication.

Duration: 15 minutes per presentation
Team Size: Individual or Pair

Prototype Parade

An exhibition-style competition where teams present working prototypes of innovative electronic devices or systems. Projects will be judged on innovation, technical complexity, practical application, and presentation quality.

Materials: Bring your own prototype
Team Size: 2-4 members

Bug Slay

A debugging challenge where participants must identify and fix errors in provided electronic circuits or code snippets. Test your troubleshooting skills under time pressure and compete to solve the most bugs accurately.

Duration: 90 minutes
Team Size: Individual

Brain Byte Brawl

A multi-round technical quiz competition testing knowledge in electronics, communications, programming, and current technological trends. Preliminary written round followed by an exciting on-stage final for top teams.

Rounds: Prelims + Finals
Team Size: 2 members

DeCode-X

A competitive coding challenge focusing on algorithm design and implementation. Participants will solve a series of increasingly difficult programming problems with constraints on time and computational efficiency.

Languages: C, C++, Python, Java
Team Size: Individual or Pair

NON-TECHNICAL EVENTS

Link Storm

A fast-paced word association game where teams must create chains of related words. Each team member must continue the chain by connecting a new word to the previous one, creating the longest possible sequence without breaks.

Duration: 3 rounds of 10 minutes each
Team Size: 3-4 members

BeatFusion

A music and rhythm-based competition where participants showcase their talent in beatboxing, rapping, or musical performance. Contestants can perform solo or in groups, with judging based on creativity, rhythm, and audience engagement.

Performance Time: 3-5 minutes
Participation: Individual or Group

Click a Story

A photography contest where participants must capture and present a series of images that tell a compelling story. Photos can be taken on campus during the event or submitted from previous work, with themes announced on the day.

Submission: 3-5 photos with caption
Team Size: Individual
